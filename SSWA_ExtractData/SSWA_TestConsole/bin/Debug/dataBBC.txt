    <h1 class="story-body__h1">Why so many celebrities have died in 2016</h1>

        <div class="byline">
        <span class="byline__name">By Roland Hughes</span>
        <span class="byline__title">BBC News</span>
    </div>
    
    <div class="story-body__mini-info-list-and-share">

</ul>

        </figure><p class="story-body__introduction">We are only four months in, but it's already been a dark, dark 2016.</p><p>It now seems rare for a week to pass without a significant celebrity death being reported - from David Bowie in the second week of January, to actor Alan Rickman a week later, to comedian Victoria Wood and <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-us-canada-36106778" class="story-body__link">Prince this week</a>.</p><p>"Enough, 2016" and a more vulgar alternative are phrases people are uttering more and more regularly.  So is this wave of celebrity deaths the new normal?</p><p>The answer is yes, according to the BBC's obituary editor Nick Serpell, who ought to know about such things.</p><p>He says the number of significant deaths this year has been "phenomenal".</p><div id="bbccom_mpu_1_2_3" class="bbccom_slot mpu-ad" aria-hidden="true">
    <div class="bbccom_advert">
        
    </div>
</div><p>Looking at the basic statistics, there's a very clear upward trend. Nick prepares obituaries for BBC television, radio and online, that run once a notable person's death is confirmed.</p><p>The number of his obituaries used across BBC outlets in recent years has leaped considerably.</p><div id="ns_chart_celebdeaths">

<p>It's a jump from only <strong>five</strong> between January and late March 2012 to a staggering <strong>24</strong> in the same period this year - <strong>an almost five-fold increase</strong>. And that's before counting some of the notable deaths in April, including American singer Merle Haggard, the former drug smuggler Howard Marks and this week's two notable departures.</p>                                                                                                    <p><a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p03rdjhm" class="story-body__link">Listen: Are there more celebrity deaths these days?</a></p><p>But might it just be that the BBC has increased its store of obituaries to such an extent it means plenty more are being used?</p><p>There are indeed more obituaries in the BBC's files - some 1,500 in total - than when Nick started 10 years ago, he says. He adds a few more every week.</p><p>But look elsewhere and the picture bears out.</p><figure class="media-with-caption">
        <div class="media-player-wrapper">
            <figure class="js-media-player-unprocessed media-player" data-playable='{"settings":{"counterName":"news.entertainment_and_arts.story.36108133.page","edition":"Asia","pageType":"eav2","uniqueID":"36108133","ui":{"locale":{"lang":"en-gb"}},"externalEmbedUrl":"http:\/\/www.bbc.com\/news\/entertainment-arts-36108133\/embed","insideIframe":false,"statsObject":[],"playlistObject":{"title":"Victoria Wood inspired a new generation of female comics in a hugely successful career on television and on stage.","holdingImageURL":null,"guidance":null,"simulcast":false,"liveRewind":false,"embedRights":"blocked","items":[{"vpid":"p03rp1bf","live":false,"duration":246,"kind":"programme"}],"summary":"Victoria Wood inspired a new generation of female comics in a hugely successful career on television and on stage."}},"otherSettings":{"advertisingAllowed":true,"continuousPlayCfg":{"enabled":false},"isAutoplayOnForAudience":false,"unProcessedImageUrl":"http:\/\/ichef-1.bbci.co.uk\/news\/640\/cpsprodpb\/16A47\/production\/_89334729_89334728.jpg"}}'></figure>
        </div>
    <figcaption class="media-with-caption__caption"><span class="off-screen">Media caption</span>Victoria Wood inspired a new generation of female comics in a hugely successful career on television and on stage.</figcaption>
</figure><p>Here in the UK, the Daily Telegraph maintains a gallery of famous people who have died, and updates it throughout the year. Up to this time in 2014, the number of those in the gallery was <strong>38</strong>. By this time last year, the number of people in the gallery was <strong>30</strong>. <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/news/culture-stars-who-died-in-2016/" class="story-body__link-external">This year</a>, the number is already <strong>75</strong>.</p><p>At the beginning of every year, the (rather morbid) website <a href="http://deathlist.net/?y=2016" class="story-body__link-external">deathlist.net</a> lists 50 celebrities it believes may pass away that year. In six of the last 10 years, two or fewer of its predictions had come true by this time - this year, five names have died so far.</p><p>This all invites the question: why?</p><p>There are a few reasons, Nick Serpell says.</p><p>"People who started becoming famous in the 1960s are now entering their 70s and are starting to die," he says.</p><p>"There are also more famous people than there used to be," he says. "In my father or grandfather's generation, the only famous people really were from cinema - there was no television.</p><p>"Then, if anybody wasn't on TV, they weren't famous."</p><h2 class="story-body__crosshead">The baby-boom factor</h2><figure class="media-with-caption">
        <div class="media-player-wrapper">
            <figure class="js-media-player-unprocessed media-player" data-playable='{"settings":{"counterName":"news.entertainment_and_arts.story.36108133.page","edition":"Asia","pageType":"eav2","uniqueID":"36108133","ui":{"locale":{"lang":"en-gb"}},"externalEmbedUrl":"http:\/\/www.bbc.com\/news\/entertainment-arts-36108133\/embed","insideIframe":false,"statsObject":[],"playlistObject":{"title":"The many faces of David Bowie","holdingImageURL":null,"guidance":null,"simulcast":false,"liveRewind":false,"embedRights":"blocked","items":[{"vpid":"p03f5nnn","live":false,"duration":209,"kind":"programme"}],"summary":"The many faces of David Bowie"}},"otherSettings":{"advertisingAllowed":true,"continuousPlayCfg":{"enabled":false},"isAutoplayOnForAudience":false,"unProcessedImageUrl":"http:\/\/ichef.bbci.co.uk\/news\/640\/cpsprodpb\/3715\/production\/_87610141_976x549-promo.jpg"}}'></figure>
        </div>
    <figcaption class="media-with-caption__caption"><span class="off-screen">Media caption</span>The many faces of David Bowie</figcaption>
</figure><p>Many of those now dying belonged to the so-called baby-boom generation, born between 1946 and 1964, that saw a huge growth in population. In the US for example, the census bureau said that <strong>76m</strong> people in 2014 belonged to the baby boomer generation - some <strong>23%</strong> of the population.</p><p>Here in the UK, people aged 65 or older make up almost 18% of the population - a 47% increase on forty years ago.</p><p>With more babies born into the baby-boom generation, it meant more went on to eventually become famous.</p><p>Now, those famous former babies, aged between 70 and 52, are dying.</p><p>The age-bracket 65 to 69 is the one, in the UK for example, where death rates really start to increase - some <strong>14.2 per 1,000 men</strong> in that age bracket died in 2014, compared with <strong>9.4 per 1,000</strong> in the 60 to 64 age bracket.</p><p>Among the major deaths this year, many - including Prince (57), Alan Rickman (69), David Bowie (69) and Victoria Wood (62) - were baby-boomers.</p><p><a href="/news/entertainment-arts-12494821" class="story-body__link">Obituary: David Bowie</a></p><h2 class="story-body__crosshead">What makes a celebrity?</h2><p>Another factor that may play into the impression that more celebrities are dying is that we have heard of more celebrities than before.</p><p>"Over the past 10 years, social media has played a big part," Nick Serpell says.</p><p>Hours before Prince's death was announced, tributes were paid <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/36098875/former-american-professional-wwe-wrestler-and-porn-star-chyna-dies-aged-45" class="story-body__link">to the American former wrestler and porn star Chyna</a>, who died aged 45.</p><p>But the news of her death was not confined to the US - close to 400,000 tweets using the word Chyna were sent worldwide on Thursday, and interest peaked in cities such as Lagos in Nigeria and Lima in Peru.</p><p>These days, it is far easier to hear news of whether anyone has died than at any time in the past.</p><figure class="media-with-caption">
        <div class="media-player-wrapper">
            <figure class="js-media-player-unprocessed media-player" data-playable="{" settings":{"countername":"news.entertainment_and_arts.story.36108133.page","edition":"asia","pagetype":"eav2","uniqueid":"36108133","ui":{"locale":{"lang":"en-gb"}},"externalembedurl":"http:\/\/www.bbc.com\/news\/entertainment-arts-36108133\/embed","insideiframe":false,"statsobject":[],"playlistobject":{"title":"a="" look="" back="" at="" some="" of="" alan="" rickman's="" most="" memorable="" roles","holdingimageurl":null,"guidance":null,"simulcast":false,"liverewind":false,"embedrights":"blocked","items":[{"vpid":"p03fhk3t","live":false,"duration":116,"kind":"programme"}],"summary":"a="" look="" back="" at="" some="" of="" alan="" rickman's="" most="" memorable="" roles"}},"othersettings":{"advertisingallowed":true,"continuousplaycfg":{"enabled":false},"isautoplayonforaudience":false,"unprocessedimageurl":"http:\/\/ichef-1.bbci.co.uk\/news\/640\/cpsprodpb\/9f4d\/production\/_87718704_bpjhhj.jpg"}}"=""></figure>
        </div>
    <figcaption class="media-with-caption__caption"><span class="off-screen">Media caption</span>A look back at some of Alan Rickman's most memorable roles</figcaption>
</figure><h2 class="story-body__crosshead">Will this carry on?</h2><p>The bad news? Yes, probably. "Over the next 10 years, these people will get into their 80s and it is going to continue at this level," Nick Serpell says.</p><p>"And that doesn't count the surprise deaths, when people die that shouldn't."</p><p>For the time being, the normally half-hour compilation of obituaries Nick produces for BBC News at the end of every year will be extended: this year, he says he has already been given permission to make it an hour long.</p>
